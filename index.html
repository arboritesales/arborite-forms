Spill Procedure
Spill occurs
▼
Think Safety
Do you need Protective Clothing? Is there a risk of fire? Is machinery stable? Evacuate all people to a safe muster point upwind of the spillage.
▼
Deal with Injuries
Check people involved for injuries. Treat as priority. Remove them upwind if safe. If reacted to substance, take label to doctor. Call emergency services if necessary.
▼
Stop the Spillage
Stop the source or contain any further spillage. Call for assistance if necessary.
▼
Contain the Spillage
Keep the spillage as small an area as possible by blocking drains, making dams and using absorbent materials including soil.
▼
Clean up the Spillage
Use proper absorbent materials to soak up the spill. Used Spill Kits etc. must be properly disposed of.
▼
Inform and Record
Contact the main office ASAP. Contamination of watercourses where there is a requirement to inform organisations such as the EA, will be dealt with by our office. Complete and submit an adverse event report form.
Fire Procedure
Fire occurs
▼
Think Safety
Evacuate all people to a safe upwind position.
▼
Raise the Alarm
Call 999 or 112 using the nearest telephone. Ask for the fire brigade. Using the Emergency Information on the site paperwork, provide as much information as you can to help assist in providing direction and location.
▼
Fire Fighting
Only fight a fire when it is appropriate to do so, and an individual is not placing themselves in greater danger. Remember PASS — Pull the pin, Aim the hose, Squeeze the lever, Sweep the hose. Put people first, and property second.
▼
Inform and Record
Contact the main office ASAP. Complete and submit an incident report form.
Medical Emergency Procedure
Medical Emergency Occurs
▼
Trained site First Aiders to administer First Aid and assess condition of casualty
▼
Only if it is safe and appropriate to do so, move the casualty take them to the nearest appropriate hospital
▼
If necessary, call 999 or 112 using the nearest telephone. Using the Emergency Information on the site paperwork, provide as much information as you can to help assist in providing direction and location along with details of the casualty
▼
Contact the main office ASAP
Office shall determine and make contact with next of kin if required
▼
Complete and submit an accident report form
Contact with Utilities Procedure
Contact with utilities
▼
Do not touch any exposed or damaged utility, do not attempt to untangle any form of wire or cable from machinery
▼
Do not attempt any form of repair
▼
Do not smoke or allow naked flames
▼
Assume that any wires are live, even if they are not arcing or sparking, or if they otherwise appear to be dead
▼
Remember that, even if lines are dead, they may be switched back on either automatically after a few seconds or remotely after a few minutes or even hours if the line's owner is not aware that their line has been damaged
▼
Contact the relevant service national emergency number immediately to inform them of what has happened
▼
If required, call the emergency services. Give them your location, tell them what has happened and that utilities are involved
▼
If you are in contact with, or close to, a damaged service, move away as quickly as possible and stay away until the service owner advises that the situation has been made safe
▼
If you are in a vehicle that has touched a wire, either stay in the vehicle or, if you need to get out, jump out of it as far as you can
▼
Do not touch the vehicle while standing on the ground. Do not return to the vehicle until it has been confirmed that it is safe to do so
▼
Be aware that if a live wire is touching the ground the area around it may be live. Keep a safe distance away from the wire or anything else it may be touching and keep others away
▼
Ensure that no other third party can enter the immediate vicinity, and any person affected by the event are provided with a warning
▼
Report all damage even where leaks or loss of power etc may not be evident
▼
Complete and submit an incident report form
Road Traffic Collision Procedure
Road Traffic Collision occurs
▼
Stop the Vehicle
Please remember it is against the law to drive away following an accident. Turn off the engine. Switch the hazard lights on.
▼
Check for Injuries
Check for any injuries to yourself or your passengers. If it's a minor collision and there are no injuries, make a note of it just in case. Call the police and an ambulance immediately if anyone is hurt or if the road is blocked.
▼
Remain Calm
Try to remain as calm as possible — it's normal to be shaken after an accident. Take a few deep breaths and try to take stock of the situation the best you can without losing your temper.
▼
Exchange Details
Exchange details with anyone involved in the accident (including witnesses). Get their: a. Name, b. Address, c. Telephone number, d. Vehicle registration number.
▼
Contact the main office
Contact next of kin if required. Arrange for roadside assistance/tow. Complete incident report form. Contact insurers if applicable. Do not apologise or admit responsibility for the crash until you're completely aware of what happened.
Contact with Underground Services Procedure
Contact with Underground Services
▼
Stop work immediately: Cease all excavation and any interaction with the damaged service.
▼
Do not approach: Do not touch, move, or try to fix the damaged service.
▼
Report the incident: Contact the main office, and/or the relevant utility provider or emergency services. The priority of contact is dependent upon the severity of underground contact — for example if contact was made with a gas main it should be the utility provider who is immediately called.
▼
Do not resume work: You cannot restart the work until the service owner, declare the area safe and issue an amended permit (where issued).
▼
Protect the damaged service: Ensure the damaged area is protected from further damage or contact until it is safe to proceed, ensure site cordons/exclusion zones remain in place.
Dealing with Threatening Behaviour Procedure
Confronted with Threatening Behaviour
▼
Identification of behaviour
Observe: Aggressive language, verbal abuse, intimidation, physical threats, hostile actions.
▼
Stay calm and assess the risk
Remain composed and avoid escalation. You need to quickly assess if there is an immediate risk of harm.
▼
Is there immediate danger present?
Where you believe there is an immediate risk of harm, or escalation that you will not be able to control — call 999. Remove yourself and others to safety.
▼
No immediate danger, but behaviour is threatening
Use de-escalation techniques; calm tone, non-confrontational stance, active listening. If behaviour continues, request the person to stop or leave the area. If unresolved or you believe you are at risk of harm, call 999 (Police).
▼
Report the incident
All interactions where threatening behaviour has been encountered must be reported to the office. Complete incident report form including event details. You may be required to speak to the Police. Further support will be offered to those affected as required.
Aerial Tree Rescue Procedure
Aerial Rescue/Assistance Required
▼
Stop work, assess the situation and determine whether emergency services are required
▼
Use of personal fall protection equipment
Determine if self-rescue is possible. If possible, allow self-rescue to take place.
Use of MEWP for rescue
Deploy MEWP. If climber can communicate & is mobile: facilitate own rescue. If unable to communicate: MEWP operator attaches casualty to platform, then attaches fall protection, then disengages. Descent made.
MEWP to MEWP
Deploy second rescue MEWP close together with access doors open. Operator attaches to rescue MEWP prior to disconnecting from their own. Descent made.
Broken down / unable to descend MEWP
Lower using ground controls, emergency power pack or manual bleed down systems. If available, use another MEWP or tree structure/personal fall protection. Contact service engineer/hire company.
▼
Deal with the aftermath:
a. Contact the main office
b. Complete accident/incident report forms
c. Quarantine the site/preserve the scene as necessary
Definitions
Accident
Any unplanned event that resulted in injury or ill health of people.
Incident
Damage or loss to property, plant, materials or the environment.
Near Miss
Any event which, under slightly different circumstances, may have resulted in injury or ill health of people or damage/loss to property, plant, materials or the environment.
